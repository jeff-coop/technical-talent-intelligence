# Platform Engineering

This page is my attempt to better understand platform engineering, the problems platform teams solve, and why platform engineering has become one of the fastest growing areas within modern software organizations.

Platform engineering sits at the intersection of infrastructure, developer productivity, cloud engineering, reliability, and internal tooling.

The goal is not to become a platform engineer.

The goal is to better understand the environments, challenges, and technical concepts that shape platform engineering talent.

---

# What Is Platform Engineering?

Platform engineering focuses on building internal platforms, tools, and services that help software engineers build, deploy, test, and operate software more efficiently.

Platform engineers create systems that enable other engineers to move faster.

Their customers are often other engineers inside the company.

---

# Why Platform Engineering Exists

As engineering organizations grow, complexity increases.

Engineers spend more time managing:

* Infrastructure
* Deployments
* CI/CD
* Kubernetes
* Cloud Resources
* Internal Tooling

Platform teams help reduce that complexity.

The goal is to create self-service systems that make developers more productive.

# Platform Engineering vs Infrastructure Engineering

## Infrastructure Engineering

Focus:

Building and operating foundational systems.

Examples:

* Databases
* Networking
* Storage
* Distributed Systems

---

## Platform Engineering

Focus:

Improving developer productivity and engineering workflows.

Examples:

* Internal Platforms
* Deployment Systems
* Developer Experience
* Engineering Enablement
