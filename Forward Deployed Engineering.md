# Forward Deployed Engineering

This page is my attempt to better understand forward deployed engineering, the problems forward deployed engineers solve, and why this role has become increasingly important at software companies that deploy complex technology into customer environments.

Forward deployed engineering sits at the intersection of software engineering, product, implementation, consulting, and customer success.

The goal is not to become a forward deployed engineer.

The goal is to better understand the environments, responsibilities, and skills that make these engineers successful.

---

# What Is Forward Deployed Engineering?

Forward deployed engineers work directly with customers to help deploy, configure, customize, integrate, and scale technical solutions.

Unlike traditional software engineers who primarily build internal products, forward deployed engineers often work at the boundary between the product and the customer.

They help ensure technology creates real-world outcomes.

---

# Why Forward Deployed Engineering Exists

Many software platforms are powerful but complex.

Customers often need help with:

* Integrations
* Data Pipelines
* Workflows
* Infrastructure
* Custom Applications
* Change Management

Forward deployed engineers help bridge the gap between technical capabilities and customer outcomes.

# Why Recruiters Should Understand Forward Deployed Engineering

Forward deployed engineering can be difficult to recruit for because it sits between multiple disciplines.

Strong candidates often combine:

* Engineering Skills
* Customer Skills
* Product Thinking
* Execution Ability

Understanding the role helps recruiters:

* Identify transferable backgrounds
* Assess technical depth
* Understand customer-facing impact
* Differentiate from traditional software engineering
