# Technical Recruiting Playbook

This page outlines principles, frameworks, and observations I've developed while recruiting technical talent.

The goal is not to create a rigid process.

Every company, team, manager, and candidate is different.

Instead, this playbook represents how I think about technical recruiting and talent acquisition.

---

# Recruiting Is An Information Problem

Most recruiting challenges can be traced back to missing information.

Examples:

* Unclear requirements
* Misaligned expectations
* Incomplete market understanding
* Poor candidate communication

My first goal is usually to improve information flow between all stakeholders.

---

# Understand The Problem Before Finding The Candidate

The best recruiting starts with understanding the business problem.

Questions I try to answer:

* Why does this role exist?
* Why now?
* What problem will this person solve?
* What does success look like?
* What would make someone exceptional in this role?

Job descriptions rarely answer these questions completely.

---

# Talent Mapping Before Outreach

Before sourcing candidates, I want to understand:

* Competitor companies
* Similar organizations
* Technical ecosystems
* Universities
* Open source communities
* Industry trends

The goal is to understand where talent is likely to be found.

---

# Focus On Signals, Not Keywords

Keywords are useful.

Signals are better.

Examples:

### Weak Signal

* Technology Keyword

### Strong Signal

* Ownership
* Open Source Contributions
* Research
* Leadership
* Technical Depth
* Learning Velocity

Strong candidates often demonstrate multiple signals.

---

# Curiosity Matters

One pattern I continue to notice among exceptional candidates:

Curiosity.

Examples:

* Side Projects
* Open Source
* Technical Writing
* Teaching
* Research
* Experimentation

Curiosity often predicts future growth.

---

# Ownership Matters

Ownership consistently appears among strong candidates.

Examples:

* Building Systems
* Leading Initiatives
* Solving Ambiguous Problems
* Mentoring Others

Titles matter less than impact.

---

# Candidate Experience Matters

Every interaction reflects on the company.

My goals:

* Clear communication
* Timely updates
* Honest feedback
* Respect for candidate time

Strong candidate experience benefits everyone.

---

# Technical Recruiting Requires Technical Learning

I do not believe recruiters need to become engineers.

I do believe recruiters should invest in understanding:

* Technical Concepts
* Engineering Organizations
* Infrastructure
* AI
* Product Development
* Technical Ecosystems

The better I understand the environment, the better I can understand the talent.

---

# Long-Term Relationships

Most candidates are not immediate hires.

Many of the strongest hires happen after months or years of relationship building.

I try to think beyond the current opening.

---

# What I'm Continuing To Learn

Questions I'm interested in:

* How does exceptional technical talent develop?
* What signals matter most?
* How can talent be identified earlier?
* What environments accelerate growth?
* What separates good recruiters from exceptional recruiters?

The answers continue to evolve.
