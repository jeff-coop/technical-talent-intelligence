# Technical Talent Intelligence Framework

This page outlines the framework I use to think about technical talent.

The goal is not to create a rigid scorecard or ranking system.

Instead, this framework helps me better understand how engineers develop, which experiences matter most, and what signals may indicate long-term potential.

Exceptional talent comes from many different backgrounds.

No single company, school, internship, or credential guarantees success.

I view talent as a combination of experiences, skills, behaviors, and environments that compound over time.

---

# Pillar 1: Technical Depth

Technical depth reflects an individual's understanding of their domain.

Examples:

* Distributed Systems
* Databases
* AI Infrastructure
* Machine Learning
* Security
* Networking
* Platform Engineering

Questions:

* How deeply do they understand their area?
* Have they solved difficult technical problems?
* Can they explain tradeoffs?
* Have they operated systems at scale?

---

# Pillar 2: Ownership

Ownership is one of the strongest indicators of future growth.

Examples:

* Built systems from scratch
* Led migrations
* Owned critical services
* Improved reliability
* Improved developer productivity
* Solved ambiguous problems

Questions:

* Do they take initiative?
* Do they drive outcomes?
* Do they create leverage for others?

---

# Pillar 3: Learning Velocity

Technology changes quickly.

The ability to learn often matters more than current knowledge.

Signals:

* Self-learning
* Side projects
* Open source
* Research
* Certifications
* Technical writing

Questions:

* How quickly can they learn?
* How do they approach unfamiliar problems?

---

# Pillar 4: Curiosity

Curiosity frequently appears among exceptional engineers.

Signals:

* Open source contributions
* Research
* Teaching
* Writing
* Experimentation
* Building projects

Questions:

* What do they learn outside of work?
* What are they curious about?

---

# Pillar 5: Communication

Strong engineers communicate clearly.

Examples:

* Technical writing
* Mentorship
* Documentation
* Presentations
* Teaching

Questions:

* Can they explain complexity simply?
* Can they influence others?

---

# Pillar 6: Leadership

Leadership is not limited to management.

Examples:

* Technical Leadership
* Mentorship
* Community Leadership
* Team Leadership
* Entrepreneurship

Questions:

* Have they helped others succeed?
* Have they influenced decisions?

---

# Pillar 7: Environment

Environment shapes development.

Examples:

* Companies
* Universities
* Research Labs
* Open Source Communities
* Fellowships
* Internships

Questions:

* What opportunities did they have?
* What ecosystems helped them grow?

---

# Signals I Pay Attention To

Examples:

* Research
* Open Source
* Publications
* Internships
* Competitive Programming
* Technical Leadership
* Teaching
* Entrepreneurship
* Community Involvement

No signal is perfect.

Multiple signals together often tell a more complete story.

---

# What This Framework Is Not

This framework is not:

* A ranking system
* A candidate scorecard
* A replacement for interviews
* A prediction model

It is simply a way to organize observations and better understand how technical talent develops.
