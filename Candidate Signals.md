# Candidate Signals

This page tracks patterns and signals that may indicate strong technical talent.

The goal is not to judge candidates by one keyword, school, company, or credential.

The goal is to better understand the experiences that often show up among high-performing engineers, technical leaders, founders, researchers, and operators.

## Technical Signals

- Open-source maintainer
- Meaningful open-source contributor
- Built production systems
- Owned critical infrastructure
- Improved reliability, latency, scale, or developer productivity
- Conference speaker
- Patent holder
- Technical writer
- Creator of developer tools or libraries

## Academic Signals

- Strong computer science program
- Teaching Assistant
- Research Assistant
- Published research
- Thesis or independent research
- Academic awards
- Fellowships or scholarships

## Competition Signals

- ICPC
- IOI
- USACO
- Codeforces
- TopCoder
- Putnam
- Meta Hacker Cup
- Hackathon winner or finalist

## Leadership Signals

- Founder
- Team captain
- Club president
- Military officer
- Eagle Scout
- Student government
- Volunteer leadership

## Ownership Signals

- Built something from scratch
- Led a migration
- Owned a platform
- Supported production systems
- Operated in ambiguous environments
- Took initiative outside normal scope

## What I Am Trying To Learn

- Which signals are most predictive of technical depth
- Which signals show ownership
- Which signals show curiosity
- Which signals show leadership
- Which signals show strong learning ability
