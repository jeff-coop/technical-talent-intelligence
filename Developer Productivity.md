# Developer Productivity

This page is my attempt to better understand developer productivity, the problems developer productivity teams solve, and why many of the strongest engineering organizations invest heavily in improving how engineers work.

Developer productivity sits at the intersection of software engineering, platform engineering, infrastructure, tooling, automation, and developer experience.

The goal is not to become a developer productivity engineer.

The goal is to better understand the systems, tools, and initiatives that help engineering organizations operate more effectively at scale.

---

# What Is Developer Productivity?

Developer productivity focuses on improving how software engineers build, test, deploy, debug, and maintain software.

The objective is simple:

Help engineers spend more time building products and less time fighting tools, infrastructure, processes, or inefficiencies.

Developer productivity teams create leverage across an entire engineering organization.

---

# Why Developer Productivity Matters

As organizations grow:

* Codebases become larger
* Systems become more complex
* Engineering teams become larger
* Deployments become more frequent

Without investment in developer productivity:

* Build times increase
* Deployments slow down
* Onboarding becomes difficult
* Engineering velocity decreases

Developer productivity helps address these challenges.

# Common Metrics

Organizations often measure:

## Build Times

How long engineers wait for builds.

## Deployment Frequency

How often teams deploy.

## Lead Time

How quickly code moves from development to production.

## Failure Rate

How often deployments create issues.

## Developer Satisfaction

How engineers feel about their tooling and workflows.
